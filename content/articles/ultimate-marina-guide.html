Marina Life

The Ultimate Cruiser's Guide
to Valentines Marina

⚓
By Captain Dave Rolle, Dockmaster
· April 8, 2026 · 6 min read · Updated for 2026 Season
⚓

If you're planning to bring your boat to Harbour Island, you've already made one of the best decisions of your cruising life. And if Valentines Marina is your destination — welcome home. I've been the Dockmaster here for twenty years, and I've watched thousands of sailors, powerboaters, and trawler captains tie up at our docks. Every single one of them eventually says the same thing: "We didn't stay long enough."

This guide covers everything you need to know before arrival — approach waypoints, slip sizes, what we have dockside, provisioning options, and the best anchorages for boats that can't squeeze into the marina.

Getting Here: Approach & Waypoints

Valentines Marina sits on the protected western (harbour) side of Harbour Island, Eleuthera, Bahamas. This is a key point — you are NOT approaching from the Atlantic/Pink Sand Beach side. The marina faces the shallow bay between Harbour Island and Eleuthera.

The standard approach from the south (coming up from Nassau or the Exumas) takes you through the North Eleuthera Channel. Coming from the north or east, most cruisers swing around the top of Eleuthera. Either way, your final approach is from the west, crossing the shallow bay.

📡 Valentines Marina Waypoints

Outer waypoint: 25°30.800'N / 076°38.400'W — approach from the southwest
Marina entrance: 25°30.350'N / 076°38.700'W
Fuel dock: Call VHF Channel 16 when 1nm out. We'll have a dock hand ready.

The approach channel has a controlling depth of approximately 6–7 feet at mean low water, with some areas shallower at the edges. We strongly recommend approaching at or near high tide if you draw more than 5.5 feet. Call us on VHF 16 before entry and we'll give you a real-time depth report and guide you in if needed.

"The first time we came in, I was nervous about the depth. I called ahead on 16, Dave talked us in personally, and we touched nothing. That set the tone for the whole visit."

— S/V Morning Star, Nordhavn 47, from their cruising blog

Slip Sizes & Availability

Valentines Marina has over 50 slips accommodating vessels from small day boats to superyachts up to 160 feet. Here's a general breakdown:

  • Slips A–F (Small): Up to 35 feet, beam up to 12 feet
  • Slips G–P (Medium): 35–55 feet, beam up to 16 feet
  • Slips Q–Z (Large): 55–80 feet, beam up to 22 feet
  • Megayacht Dock: Up to 160 feet LOA — call ahead for arrangement
  • End-ties: Available for wide-beam trawlers and catamarans

Reservations are strongly recommended from November through April (peak season) and for the Easter/Regatta weeks. In shoulder season (May–October) we almost always have walk-in slips available, but calling ahead is never a bad idea.

Marina Amenities & Services

We've invested heavily in making Valentines the best-equipped marina in the northern Bahamas. Here's what you'll find dockside:

  • 30A and 50A shore power at every slip (100A available at large slips)
  • Fresh water hookup at every berth
  • Fuel dock: diesel and gasoline, competitive Bahamian pricing
  • Pump-out service available Mon–Sat
  • High-speed marina Wi-Fi (surprisingly good)
  • Clean, air-conditioned bathrooms, showers, and laundry
  • Ice machine (24-hour access)
  • Marina store with basic hardware, lines, fenders, and snacks
  • Concierge service: customs clearance, provisioning runs, ferry bookings

Customs & Immigration

If Valentines is your first Bahamian port of entry, we can help. Call us before you depart the US or prior Bahamas island and we'll have the paperwork ready. The customs and immigration officials visit the marina directly — no need to go to the government dock. We've done this thousands of times.

Key documents you'll need: ship's papers, passports for all aboard, fishing permit if applicable, and your completed Bahamas Customs Declaration (CFIS form, available online or onboard).

Provisioning & Supplies

Harbour Island is a small island, and provisioning here is more expensive than the US. That said, here's what's available within walking distance or a short golf-cart ride:

  • Johnson's Grocery: Dunmore Town — best selection on island, prices fair for Bahamas
  • Valentines Marina Store: Emergency hardware, fishing tackle, ice, cold drinks
  • Local Fish Market: Fresh-caught snapper, grouper, and conch — buy direct from the fishermen at the dock, usually afternoons
  • Dunmore Bakery: Fresh bread and pastries every morning — the cinnamon rolls are worth setting an alarm for

For major provisioning, we recommend stopping at Nassau (Nassau Yacht Haven or Atlantis Marina) before coming to Harbour Island, or getting provisions delivered from Eleuthera via the ferry. Call the marina ahead and we can arrange a grocery run with our concierge service for a small fee.

Best Nearby Anchorages

Can't get a slip or prefer to anchor? Here are the spots our regulars use:

  • Harbour Island Bay (North): Best protection in northerly and easterly winds, 6–8ft MLW, good holding in sand. Short dinghy ride to the town dock.
  • Eleuthera East Side (Glass Window): In calm conditions only — beautiful but completely exposed to Atlantic swell. Day stop only.
  • Royal Island: Uninhabited island about 5nm north. Excellent protection, beautiful anchorage, good snorkelling. A Valentines favourite for a night away.

🌀 Hurricane Season Note

If you're planning to leave your boat in the Bahamas during hurricane season (June–November), Valentines Marina does not offer long-term storage. The best hurricane holes in the area are the Exumas (Staniel Cay, Black Point) and the western Eleuthera mangroves. Call us and we'll point you to the right places for your boat's needs.

Getting Around Harbour Island

No cars on Harbour Island — it's golf carts and feet. Rental golf carts are available from several operators in Dunmore Town (ask our concierge). The main attractions are all walkable from the town dock:

  • Pink Sand Beach — 15-minute walk across the island, or 5 minutes by golf cart
  • Dunmore Town — the historic settlement with restaurants, shops, and bars
  • Valentines Restaurant — right here on the marina!

The ferry to North Eleuthera (and onward by taxi to the airport or south Eleuthera) runs regularly and takes about 10 minutes. Ask at the marina office for the current schedule.

Ready to Come In?

We'd love to have you. Call us on VHF Channel 16 when you're an hour out and we'll have a slip ready, a dock hand on the dock, and a cold Kalik waiting. That's the Valentines promise.

See you on the water. 🌊
