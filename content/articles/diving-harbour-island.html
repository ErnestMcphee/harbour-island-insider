Diving

Top 7 Dive Sites
Around Harbour Island

🤿
By Marcus Thompson, PADI Course Director
· March 15, 2026 · 7 min read · Updated for 2026
🤿

I've been diving these waters for seventeen years, and I still get excited every single time the boat slows down and the anchor goes over. Harbour Island sits at the junction of the Atlantic Ocean and the Caribbean Sea, which creates a remarkable variety of underwater environments in a very small area — coral walls, drift dives, blue holes, shallow gardens, and the occasional encounter with a creature that will stop your heart.

Here are the seven sites I recommend most often, for every level from brand-new snorkeller to technical diver chasing the deep wall.

🤿 Dive with Valentines Dive Centre

All seven sites below are accessible from our PADI-certified dive centre, located right on the marina. We run two-tank morning dives daily, plus afternoon specialty dives, night dives, and custom charters. Equipment rental and PADI courses available. Book at the front desk or call +1 (242) 333-2142.

The Dive Sites

1

Current Cut

Advanced Max depth: 60 ft Drift Dive

The jewel of Eleuthera diving. Current Cut is a narrow channel between North Eleuthera and Current Island, and when the tide runs — which it does with force — it creates one of the most exhilarating drift dives in the Western Hemisphere. You drop in, the current catches you, and you fly through a corridor of hard coral absolutely packed with reef fish, eagle rays, nurse sharks, and (if you're lucky) a hammerhead cruising the deeper edge.

Timing is everything. We run Current Cut twice daily at slack current — about 45 minutes before peak flow. Hit it wrong and it's undiveable. Hit it right and it's the dive of your life. This is advanced territory: strong current experience required.

2

The Plateau

Beginner Friendly Max depth: 35 ft

Our most-dived site and the perfect introduction to Harbour Island's underwater world. The Plateau is a broad coral shelf running parallel to the island's Atlantic shore, starting at about 15 feet and dropping gradually to 35. The coral health here is exceptional — fan corals, brain corals, and elk horn formations that you simply don't see like this anymore.

Regulars know to look under the large brain coral heads for sleeping nurse sharks, and the cleaning stations attract an extraordinary variety of reef fish. On a morning dive, the light comes down in shafts through the clear water and makes this feel like swimming through a cathedral.

3

The Blue Hole

Intermediate Max depth: 80 ft

About two miles north of the marina lies a submerged sinkhole — a blue hole — that drops through a coral reef into what appears to be a bottomless indigo abyss. Swimming over the edge and looking down is one of the most vertigo-inducing and awe-inspiring things you can do underwater.

The walls of the blue hole are covered in black coral, sea fans, and basket sponges. Turtles frequently rest on the shallow rim. The cool upwelling from the depth below attracts pelagic fish along the edges. We allow certified divers with deep-dive experience to descend to 80 feet; beyond that requires technical certification.

4

Pink Sand Reef

Snorkel-Friendly Max depth: 20 ft

Running directly offshore from the southern end of Pink Sand Beach, this shallow reef is accessible by snorkel from the shore — you don't even need a boat. The reef starts at waist depth and the coral is alive with parrotfish, sergeant majors, queen angelfish, and the occasional spotted eagle ray.

This is where we bring our beginner divers and open-water students. In the afternoons, when the day-trippers are gone, you can have this reef entirely to yourself. I've found juvenile seahorses clinging to sea grass here on three different occasions — a sign of exceptional reef health.

5

The Pinnacles

Intermediate Max depth: 55 ft

A series of coral columns rising from a sandy bottom at 55 feet to within 20 feet of the surface, the Pinnacles create a maze of swim-throughs, overhangs, and hiding spots. Moray eels are common here — we've spotted the same enormous green moray in the same crevice for eleven years.

Blacktip reef sharks patrol the sandy channels between the pinnacles, usually in groups of three to five. They're curious but not aggressive. Keep your hands to yourself, move slowly, and they'll give you a close pass that will absolutely make your dive.

6

The Train Wreck

Intermediate Max depth: 45 ft

Yes — a train wreck. In the late 1800s, a barge carrying two steam locomotives sank in shallow water off the western shore of Eleuthera. The locomotives lie on their sides in 40–45 feet of crystal-clear water, now completely encrusted in coral and sponge, forming one of the most surreal and photogenic dive sites in the entire Caribbean.

Schools of yellowtail snapper and grunt swirl around the old iron boilers. Lionfish (an invasive species we actively cull) take shelter in the hollow engine compartments. Underwater photographers: bring a wide-angle lens and plan to use your entire tank here.

7

Valentines Wall

Advanced Max depth: 130 ft+

Our house reef has a secret: about a quarter mile north, the plateau drops away into a sheer vertical wall that falls well past recreational diving limits. Valentines Wall is our favourite site for experienced divers — a hang-and-drift along the face of a coral cliff, with massive barrel sponges, black coral trees, and pelagic visitors sweeping in from the open ocean.

Whale sharks have been spotted here in March and April. Oceanic whitetip sharks are occasional visitors in winter. Even at the shallowest recreational depths (60–80 feet), this wall is breathtaking. If you're a technical diver, let us know — we can discuss what's waiting deeper.

Planning Your Dives with Valentines

Our dive centre is open 7 days a week. We run a two-tank morning dive departing at 8am and a one-tank afternoon dive departing at 2pm. Night dives run on Tuesdays and Thursdays (conditions permitting). We keep groups small — maximum 6 divers per instructor — so you always get personal attention and the site stays unspoiled.

  • PADI courses: Discover Scuba (no experience needed), Open Water, Advanced Open Water, Rescue Diver, and Divemaster. Ask about our multi-day course packages.
  • Equipment rental: Full sets available; BCD, regulator, wetsuit, mask, fins, tank, and weights included in dive package prices.
  • Nitrox: Available for certified Enriched Air divers — extends your bottom time considerably at 35–55 feet.
  • Underwater photography: We offer rental GoPros and guided photo dives. Ask about our photo workshop weekends.
