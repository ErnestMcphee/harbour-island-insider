Travel Tips

Getting to Harbour Island:
Every Route Explained

✈️
By Aaliyah Johnson, Island Life & Travel Editor
· April 18, 2026 · 8 min read
✈️

Harbour Island's reputation for remoteness is part of its appeal — this is not the sort of place you end up at by accident. There's no international airport, no cruise ship dock, no land bridge. You have to mean it to get here. And yet once you understand the logistics, you'll realize it's genuinely not that complicated. Miami to Harbour Island can be done in under three hours. The "difficulty" of getting here is mostly mystique.

Here is every route, broken down clearly, with everything you need to know before you travel.

By Air: Fly Direct to North Eleuthera (ELH)

The most straightforward route for most visitors. North Eleuthera Airport (IATA: ELH) is a small regional airport on Eleuthera, about 15 minutes from the Harbour Island ferry dock. Several carriers fly direct:

  • American Airlines: Daily service from Miami (MIA). Flight time approximately 45 minutes. This is the most reliable and frequently booked route.
  • Silver Airways: Service from Fort Lauderdale (FLL) and sometimes Orlando. Check current schedules as routes evolve seasonally.
  • Bahamasair: Connects ELH to Nassau (NAS), which feeds into broader international connections.

From North Eleuthera Airport, the process is simple:

  1. Clear Bahamian customs and immigration at ELH (the airport handles this — it's quick).
  2. Take a taxi to the ferry dock — approximately 5 minutes, $5–8 per person.
  3. Board the water taxi to Harbour Island — the ferry runs approximately every 20 minutes throughout the day, $5 per person, 10-minute crossing.

Start to dock in Dunmore Town: under 30 minutes from wheels-down. It is one of the more painless island arrivals in the Caribbean.

✈️ ELH Booking Tip

ELH is a small airport with limited seats, and the Miami route in particular sells out weeks in advance during peak season (December through April). Book your flights as early as possible — last-minute fares are both expensive and sometimes unavailable. Christmas week and Easter week have historically sold out months ahead.

By Air from Nassau

If you're flying internationally from Europe, Canada, or beyond, Nassau Lynden Pindling International Airport (NAS) is your likely hub. British Airways flies direct from London. Delta and American serve Nassau from multiple US cities. Air Canada operates seasonal routes.

From Nassau, getting to Harbour Island means a short connecting flight:

  • Flamingo Air: Charter and scheduled service Nassau to ELH, approximately 30 minutes. Small aircraft (6–9 seats), book ahead.
  • Southern Air Charter: Similar service, similar aircraft. Prices are comparable — roughly $150–250 per person each way depending on season.
  • Bahamasair: The national carrier operates scheduled service but frequency varies — check current timetables.

Nassau-to-ELH flights are frequent enough that the same-day connection is usually manageable, but allow at least 90 minutes between your international arrival and your inter-island departure. Customs and baggage at NAS moves at its own pace.

By Boat: The Premium Way to Arrive

If you have access to a boat — your own, a charter, or a friend's — arriving at Harbour Island by sea is unquestionably the way to do it. Coming into the harbour under sail or power, with the colourful cottages of Dunmore Town rising on the hillside above the docks, is one of the better arrival experiences in the Bahamas.

The marina on the western (harbour) side of the island is Valentines. It's the natural destination for visiting boats and has for decades been the hub of the island's cruising community.

From Florida: West Palm Beach to Harbour Island is approximately 55 nautical miles — a comfortable day passage for most powerboats and an overnight or two-day trip for sailboats depending on conditions. The Gulf Stream crossing requires attention to weather windows; northerly swells stack against the current and can make for a rough ride. Plan your departure for a settled period.

From Nassau or the Exumas: Harbour Island sits at the northern tip of the Eleuthera chain, easily accessible from Nassau (approximately 55nm northeast) or from the Exumas via the Exuma Sound. The approach from Nassau passes through the Northeast Providence Channel — usually comfortable in settled trades.

Approaching the marina: Give Valentines Marina a call on VHF Channel 16 when you're an hour out. They monitor 16 continuously and will give you a current depth report for the approach channel (controlling depth approximately 6–7 feet at mean low water), assign your slip, and have a dock hand ready on the dock when you arrive. For full approach waypoints and detailed marina information, read our complete cruiser's guide to Valentines Marina.

The Ferry from Eleuthera

A less commonly used but perfectly viable option for those arriving by land transport on Eleuthera — perhaps via a rental car from Governor's Harbour Airport (GHB), or by bus from Nassau on the ferry to Eleuthera:

  • From Three Island Dock (North Eleuthera): This is the main water taxi dock for Harbour Island — the same one used by ELH arrivals. It's the fastest and most direct crossing.
  • From Spanish Wells: Spanish Wells is a small, self-contained settlement on St. George's Cay adjacent to North Eleuthera. A ferry connects to the main Eleuthera dock and onward to Harbour Island. Less direct but useful if you're combining a Spanish Wells visit.

For those driving through Eleuthera on a road trip (the island is 110 miles long and well worth exploring), the northern terminus of the highway puts you at Three Island Dock for the Harbour Island crossing.

Timing Your Trip: Seasons and Trade-offs

Peak season (December through April) brings the most reliable weather, the most direct flights, and the highest prices. The island is full — restaurants require reservations, marina slips book up months in advance, and the Pink Sand Beach sees real crowds on weekend afternoons when day-trip boats arrive from Nassau. If you want the island at its most vibrant and social, peak season delivers. Book everything well ahead.

Shoulder and off-season (May through November) offers a different experience: significantly lower rates at the resort, a quieter island, and a more local, unhurried atmosphere. The trade-off is occasional tropical weather — squalls, higher humidity, and the background awareness of hurricane season (June through November). Most seasons pass without significant storms, but travel insurance is wise. Flights may be less frequent or require connections through Nassau.

My personal favourite window: late October through mid-November. The rates are still shoulder-season, the weather has usually stabilized after the bulk of hurricane season, the island is quiet, and the light in the late afternoon is extraordinary. The reef is also at its best after a season of warm, calm water.

Practical Arrival Tips

Bahamas Customs and Immigration

All travellers to the Bahamas must complete the Bahamas Immigration/Customs Declaration (CFIS form) before arrival. The form is available online at travel.gov.bs — complete it before you fly or sail. You'll need passport details, flight or vessel information, and your accommodation address in the Bahamas.

  • Import limits (per person): 1 litre of spirits, 1 litre of wine, 200 cigarettes, $100 in gifts/souvenirs duty-free. Declare anything over these limits.
  • Currency: The Bahamian dollar is pegged 1:1 to the US dollar, and USD is accepted everywhere on the island. ATMs on Harbour Island can be unreliable — bring a mix of cash and a card with no foreign transaction fees.
  • What to declare: Fresh fruits and vegetables, meat, firearms (prohibited — don't try), and items valued over your duty-free allowance. The Bahamas customs process is generally smooth for leisure travellers arriving in good faith.
  • For boaters: If Valentines is your first Bahamian port of entry, call ahead and the marina team can assist with the customs clearance process — officials visit the marina directly.
Plan Ahead

Book Your Slip or Room Before You Depart

Whether you're arriving by air or boat, having your accommodation confirmed before you travel makes the whole journey smoother. Valentines Resort & Marina offers hotel rooms, private villas, and marina slips — all bookable in advance. In peak season, this is not optional advice.
