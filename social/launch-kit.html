Day 9 Pink Sand Science
Interesting Facts
Instagram ⏰ 7:00 PM EST
The pink sand beach is real. Here's why it's pink. 🌸🔬 We know — you've probably seen the photos and wondered if it's enhanced. It's not. And the science behind it is genuinely fascinating. The pink colour comes from broken shells and exoskeletons of a tiny organism called Foraminifera (or "Foram" for short). These microscopic creatures live in the coral reef and have bright red or pink shells. When they die, their remains wash up on shore and mix with the white sand. The result? That iconic blush-pink colour that makes Harbour Island famous. The best pink is visible: 🌅 Early morning — before footprints disturb the wet sand 🌧️ After rainfall — water saturates the colour 🌊 When the tide is low — more sand is exposed Swipe for our tips on getting the best beach photos ➡️ Full article on the beach — including where the pinkest sand is, and the best time of day — is on the blog now. 🔗 Link in bio. #pinksandbeach #pinksand #harbourisland #bahamastravel #caribbeanbeach #travelfacts #beachscience #tropicalbeach

Day 26 Photography Tips
Reels / Video
Instagram ⏰ 6:00 PM EST
How to photograph the pink sand beach so it actually looks like the pink sand beach. 📸🌸 The #1 complaint we hear from first-time visitors: "My photos don't look like everyone else's photos of the beach." Here's why — and how to fix it. The Problem: Phone cameras auto-adjust white balance and exposure, which flattens the pink. The sand looks almost white in most automatic shots. The Fix — 5 techniques that actually work: 1. 🌅 Shoot in the first or last hour of daylight. The warm orange and red tones in golden hour light amplify the pink dramatically. 2. 💧 Shoot the wet sand, not the dry sand. Where the waves retreat, the sand is saturated with water — the pink is 3x more vivid. 3. 📐 Get low. Knee level or lower. Shoot along the shoreline, not down at your feet. 4. 🎨 Shoot in RAW (if your phone allows it) — then pull up the Magenta and Red sliders in Lightroom Mobile. Free app, transformative results. 5. ☁️ Overcast days are actually better for pink sand photography. Diffused light, no harsh shadows, even colour saturation. Platform Strategy Quick Reference

Instagram — Growth Reels reach new audiences. Carousels drive saves. Stories maintain daily connection. Feed posts build the archive. Prioritise Reels in the first 90 days for account growth — the algorithm rewards new Reel creators heavily.
Facebook — Traffic Facebook drives the most blog traffic of any social platform for travel content. Post links in comments (not body text) to avoid suppression. Join 3–5 Bahamas and Caribbean travel groups and share relevant articles (check group rules first).
Pinterest — Long-Term Pinterest results compound. A pin published today may drive traffic for 3 years. Prioritise keyword-optimised pin descriptions over beautiful captions. Tall images (2:3 ratio, 1000×1500px) perform best. Post 5 pins per day minimum.
X/Twitter — Conversation X is best for real-time engagement: when news about the Bahamas breaks, when someone tweets about planning a trip, when a travel journalist mentions Harbour Island. Set up keyword alerts for "Harbour Island" and "Bahamas travel" and join those conversations daily.
Valentines Promotion Every third post (Days 3, 7, 10, 14, 17, 20, 24, 27, 30) features Valentines Resort & Marina. Always include the booking link in the link-in-bio. Track clicks separately in Linktree. Never sound like an ad — always tie it to a genuine experience or recommendation.
Analytics to Watch Instagram: saves and profile visits (not just likes). Facebook: link clicks and shares. Pinterest: outbound clicks to your blog. X: profile visits and link clicks. Monthly: compare which content type drives the most blog traffic via Google Analytics UTM parameters.
Posting Consistency Consistency beats frequency. A reliable 5-post week every week outperforms a 15-post week followed by silence. Use a scheduler (Later, Buffer, or Meta Business Suite for Facebook/Instagram) to batch content creation once a week and schedule everything in advance.
Hashtag Rotation Rotate between the three hashtag sets in Section C. Never use the same set two posts in a row. For maximum reach, put hashtags in the first comment, not the caption, and use 10–15 highly targeted tags rather than all 30 once your account passes 500 followers.
